Tiramisu

Description

Tiramisu is a classic Italian dessert made of layers of espresso-soaked ladyfingers, mascarpone cream, and cocoa powder. The ladyfingers are dipped in coffee, absorbing the bold flavors, while the mascarpone mixture adds a rich, creamy texture. Once layered, the dessert is chilled to meld the flavors, creating a balanced treat of sweetness and bitterness.

Widely popular worldwide, tiramisu is perfect for any occasion, often served in individual portions or as a trifle. Variations can include flavored liqueurs like rum or Marsala, or even fruit and chocolate for added complexity. Its indulgent yet not overly heavy qualities make it a go-to dessert for celebrations.

Ingredients

  • Ladyfingers
  • Espresso
  • Mascarpone cheese
  • Heavy cream
  • Egg yolks
  • Sugar
  • Cocoa powder
  • Dark rum or marsala

Steps

  1. Brew espresso or strong coffee and allow it to cool.
  2. Dip ladyfingers briefly into the cooled coffee, making sure they don't get too soggy.
  3. Whisk together mascarpone cheese, sugar, and egg yolks in a bowl until smooth.
  4. In a separate bowl, whip the heavy cream until stiff peaks form.
  5. Gently fold the whipped cream into the mascarpone mixture.
  6. Layer the dipped ladyfingers in the bottom of a dish or serving cups.
  7. Spread a layer of the mascarpone cream mixture over the ladyfingers.
  8. Repeat with another layer of dipped ladyfingers and mascarpone cream.
  9. Dust the top with cocoa powder.
  10. Chill in the fridge for at least 4 hours to allow the flavors to meld.
