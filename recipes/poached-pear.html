Poached Pears

Description

Poached pears are pears gently simmered in a spiced liquid, often made with wine, sugar, and cinnamon. The pears absorb the aromatic flavors, becoming tender and sweet. Bosc pears are most commonly used, though other varieties can be substituted. The poached pears are often served with whipped cream, ice cream, or the reduced poaching syrup.

This elegant dessert is easy to prepare in advance and can be customized with different poaching liquids to create various flavor profiles. Poached pears are ideal for special occasions, offering a light yet sophisticated end to a meal, served either warm or chilled.

Ingredients

  • Pears
  • Wine
  • Sugar
  • Cinnamon sticks
  • Cloves
  • Vanilla bean or extract
  • Lemon zest
  • Water

Steps

  1. Peel the pears, leaving the stems intact.
  2. In a large pot, combine wine, water, sugar, cinnamon, cloves, and lemon zest.
  3. Bring the mixture to a simmer, stirring to dissolve the sugar.
  4. Add the pears to the pot, making sure they are mostly submerged.
  5. Cover and simmer gently for 20-30 minutes, turning the pears occasionally.
  6. Check for tenderness with a forkâ€”pears should be soft but not falling apart.
  7. Remove the pears from the poaching liquid and set aside.
  8. Reduce the poaching liquid to a syrup over medium heat for 10-15 minutes.
  9. Pour the reduced syrup over the pears when serving.
  10. Serve warm or chilled, optionally with whipped cream or ice cream.
