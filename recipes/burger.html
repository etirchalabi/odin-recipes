Burger

Description

A hamburger is a ground beef patty, seasoned and grilled or pan-fried, served in a soft bun with condiments like ketchup, mustard, and pickles, plus fresh veggies. The patty is the main attraction, juicy and flavorful, and the burger can be customized with cheese, bacon, or gourmet toppings like avocado.

A global favorite, the hamburger is enjoyed in many variations, from fast food to gourmet options. It's often paired with fries or salad and can also be made with plant-based patties for vegetarian alternatives, making it a versatile and enduring comfort food.

Ingredients

  • Gound beef
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • Hamburger buns
  • Ketchup
  • Mustard
  • Pickles
  • Water
  • Lettuce
  • Tomato
  • Onion
  • Cheese

Steps

  1. Preheat the grill or a skillet over medium-high heat.
  2. Season the ground beef with salt and pepper.
  3. Shape the beef into patties, about 3/4-inch thick.
  4. Place the patties on the grill or skillet and cook for 3-5 minutes per side for medium doneness.
  5. Add cheese to the patties in the last minute of cooking if making cheeseburgers.
  6. Toast the hamburger buns lightly on the grill or in a pan.
  7. Assemble the burger by placing the cooked patty on the bottom bun.
  8. Add condiments like ketchup, mustard, and pickles.
  9. Top with lettuce, tomato, and onion if desired.
  10. Place the top bun on and serve immediately with sides of choice.
